# Factual Notes: FRWRM Grant Program RFA 2025-2026

## Program Overview
- **Program Name:** Forest Restoration and Wildfire Risk Mitigation (FRWRM) Grant Program.
- **Established:** 2017 via **Senate Bill 17-050**, consolidating the former Colorado Forest Restoration Grant Program (admin. by CSFS) and Wildfire Risk Reduction Grant Program (admin. by Colorado Dept. of Natural Resources).
- **Administering Agency:** Colorado State Forest Service (CSFS).
- **2021 Update:** Program scope was expanded and additional funding provided by the Colorado General Assembly.
- **Purpose:** Competitive, cost-share grant program supporting community-level actions to reduce wildfire risk to people, property, and infrastructure in the wildland-urban interface (WUI), promote forest health/restoration, and encourage utilization of woody material for traditional forest products and biomass energy.
- **Funding Available (2025-2026):** ~$7,040,000 total.
  - Up to $2 million may be awarded to **Capacity Building projects**.
  - Remaining funds for **Fuels and Forest Health projects**.
  - CSFS may award full or partial funding.

## Eligibility
- **Eligible Applicants:**
  - Local community groups (homeowners, neighborhood, property associations).
  - Local governments (counties, municipalities, fire protection districts, special districts).
  - Public or private utilities, including water providers.
  - State agencies.
  - Non-profits promoting hazardous fuels reduction in partnership with local/state/private entities.
  - Individual property owners only if collaborating with an above-listed entity and providing benefits beyond their own property.
- **Applicant Responsibilities:** Serve as or designate a fiscal agent (must be IRS-registered, have TIN, legal authority); confirm landowner agreement to reimbursement/reporting/monitoring; comply with federal/state environmental laws; follow **Colorado Forestry Best Management Practices (BMPs)** (alternative BMPs must be pre-approved by local CSFS Field Office).
- **Project Requirements:**
  - All grant funds must be used on land within **Colorado**.
  - If federal lands included: must maintain continuity within 1-mile across landscape; total federal acres ≤50% of total project acres.
  - Primary goal: reduce wildfire risk to people, property, infrastructure in WUI.
  - Located within/near WUI as defined by **Colorado Forest Atlas**.
  - Benefit more than one individual property.
  - Utilize saleable woody materials where applicable; remove/dispose of slash and non-merchantable material.
  - Be ready to begin first phase within the first award year (e.g., hire staff, purchase equipment, project layout, call for bids, enter contract).

## Qualifying Project Types
- **Two project types, separate applications required for each:**
  1. **Fuels and Forest Health Projects**
     - Must strategically reduce wildfire risk and promote forest health using scientifically-supported practices.
     - Activities include: creating/maintaining defensible space/fuelbreaks (per CSFS Home Ignition Zone and fuelbreak guidelines); mechanical thinning; prescribed fire; removing saleable woody materials with documented utilization plans; slash disposal (chipping, mulching, grinding, pile burning, broadcast burning, mechanical removal); reducing excessive tree competition; restoring ecosystem function, structure, species composition; preserving older/larger trees; replanting trees in deforested areas.
  2. **Capacity Building Projects**
     - Aim to increase local capacity to plan/implement fuels and forest health projects.
     - Activities: purchase/use of equipment for hazardous fuels reduction/woody biomass utilization (e.g., wood chipper for communities); outreach to plan projects (prioritization, prescription development); increased staffing for collaborative/community groups.
     - Equipment purchased must remain owned/maintained by award recipient for a minimum of **five years** post-grant.
     - Ineligible for this type: projects benefiting only one individual, permanent infrastructure, policy development/advocacy, fire preparedness/suppression equipment, education/outreach not directly tied to fuels reduction.

## Additional Program Criteria
- **Forest Products and Wood Utilization:**
  - Proposals must include a defined plan for utilizing woody materials.
  - Required consultation with wood utilization businesses/companies and **CoWood (Colorado Wood Utilization and Marketing)**.
  - Contact CoWood at **csfs_cowood@colostate.edu** with project description, name, location, and applicant entity. CSFS field offices also provide technical expertise.
- **Strategic Proposals:** must maximize effectiveness; preferences include:
  1. Implementation across land ownership boundaries.
  2. Projects within higher priority areas from **2020 Colorado State Forest Action Plan**. Applicants **must** include a 1-page map showing **Composite Priority theme** and **Social Vulnerability Index**. Instructions linked in RFA.
  3. Project areas with a completed **Community Wildfire Protection Plan (CWPP)** or similar plan (consult approved CWPP list).
  4. Part of a larger landscape-scale treatment effort.
  5. Proximity to recently or planned treated public lands.
- **County Level Coordination:**
  - Required coordination with county officials; identify whom consulted and outcome.
  - Must include a signed letter of support from a permissible county official: county commissioner, sheriff’s office representative, county forester/forestry staff, or county wildfire coordinator. Letter cannot be from applicant or CSFS representative.

## Additional Program Preferences
- **Youth/Veterans Partnerships:** Encouraged to use accredited **Colorado Youth Corps Association (CYCA)** organization or accredited Colorado Corps program serving veterans. Provide letter of support if partnering.
- **Protection of Water Supplies:** Preference for projects that protect water supplies through wildfire risk reduction to watersheds, water treatment/storage, and forest management (thinning, selective harvest, clearcutting, chipping, mastication, replanting).
- **Leveraged Financial Resources:** Projects should substantially leverage additional funds (described in budget narrative).
- **Adoption of Local Measures:** Preference for projects complementing local measures to reduce wildfire risk, especially stronger measures. Examples:
  - Projects from CWPP, Critical Community Watershed Wildfire Protection Plan, FEMA-approved hazard mitigation plan (plans <5 years old get greater preference).
  - Adoption of county/local building codes for wildfire mitigation, Wildfire Mitigation Overlay Zoning.
  - Participation in **Firewise USA®** program.
  - Ongoing citizen outreach/education, hazard mitigation planning, forest management planning/implementation, existing slash/mulch collection or chipping programs.

## Budget & Match Requirements
- **Matching Funds:** Minimum 1:1 match (50% match) required. Grant share ≤50%, match ≥50%.
  - **Reduced match (25% match, 3:1 grant-to-match):** If project area is partially/fully in an **area of fewer economic resources** (determined via Colorado Forest Atlas). Grant share ≤75%, match ≥25%.
- **Eligible Match Contributions:** Private, local, state, or federal support directly related to the project. State funds allowed as match, but no more than 50% of applicant’s total match may come from other state funding sources (unless applicant is a state agency). Forms: cash, in-kind (donated supplies, equipment, volunteer labor valued at **$38.74/hour per individual for 2026** – CSFS volunteer labor rate).
- **Match Documentation:** Must confirm match prior to applying. If awarded, submit verification pre-award (signed grant agreement/award letter for grant-based match, letters of commitment from landowners/partners).
- **Revenue/Cost Offsets:** Must be clearly explained and factored into grant request.
- **Invoicing:** Only actual award recipient/fiscal agent costs reimbursable. Third-party reimbursement model may be allowed if applicant lacks working capital; must outline in Payment Structure & Flow of Funds.

## Reporting, Monitoring, and Landowner Requirements
- **Reimbursement:** Occurs only after CSFS inspection confirms completed activities, accurate reporting/reimbursement docs, and proof of required match ratio.
- **Spatial Data:** Each reimbursement request must include spatial map data (e.g., shapefiles) of completed work.
- **Final Report:** Required at project completion using CSFS-provided template.
- **Participating Landowner Confirmation:** Identify and confirm landowners prior to application. If funded, submit verification pre-award (signed cost-share/intent to participate agreement, letters of commitment, spreadsheets).
- **CSFS Monitoring:** Award recipients must provide electronic map boundaries (shapefiles, KMLs); authorize CSFS site access before treatment and for at least **two years post-treatment** for monitoring effectiveness.

## Application Submission
- Submit via **email to local CSFS Field Office** by deadline.
- Packet must include: grant application, all required/supporting documents, each saved as clearly labelled PDF, combined into a single compressed zip file. Do not scan/alter application form (character counts must remain intact). No hard copies.

## Evaluation
- Applications independently scored by a **Technical Advisory Panel** convened by CSFS (mandated by SB 17-050).
- Panel recommends projects to the **State Forester**, who makes final decisions.
