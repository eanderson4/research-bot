### Program Purpose & Description
- “CAL FIRE’s Forest Health Program funds active restoration and reforestation activities aimed at providing for more resilient and sustained forests to ensure future existence of forests in California while also mitigating climate change, protecting communities from fire risk, strengthening rural economies and improving California’s water & air.”
- The program provides grants to regionally‑based partners and collaboratives to increase fuels management, fire reintroduction, treatment of degraded areas, and conservation of forests.

### Funding Limits & Scale Requirements
- **Management activity projects:** minimum grant request **$750,000**; maximum allowable request **$7 million**.
- **Landscape scale:** Projects must be large‑scale, focusing on forestlands of no less than **800 acres** (non‑contiguous allowed). Landscapes may include watersheds, firesheds, or larger logical management units with multiple landowners/jurisdictions.

### Eligible Applicants
- Local, state, and federal agencies (including federal land management agencies)
- Universities
- Special districts
- Native American tribes
- Private forest landowners
- Non‑profit 501(c)(3) or 501(c)(6) organizations (e.g., fire safe councils, land trusts)

### Greenhouse Gas (GHG) Requirements
- Must further the goals of the **Global Warming Solutions Act of 2006**.
- Applicants must include a quantitative estimate of net GHG benefit (metric tonnes CO₂e) using the **Forest Health Quantification Methodology (QM) and Calculator Tool**.
- Activities without measurable GHG benefit (planning, research, education) require a “justifiable qualitative description of how the activity will ultimately result in emissions reductions.”

### Project Readiness & Environmental Compliance
- “Preference is given to projects that have environmental compliance (CEQA, NEPA, etc.) completed before the project application is submitted.”
- Grantees must agree to complete all needed environmental compliance within **one year of award** and demonstrate progress toward completing the work within **six months of award**.

### Funding Sources & Guidelines
