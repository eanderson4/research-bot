## Dense Factual Notes

*   **Source**: U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) Water Science School, "Total Water Use in the United States," published June 8, 2018 (data year 2015). [URL](https://www.usgs.gov/water-science-school/science/total-water-use-united-states)
*   **Total U.S. water use (2015)**: ~322 billion gallons per day (Bgal/d), 9% less than in 2010 and the lowest level since before 1970.
*   **Freshwater vs. saline**: Freshwater withdrawals: 281 Bgal/d (87% of total). Saline water withdrawals: 41.0 Bgal/d (13% of total).
*   **Freshwater sources**: Surface water 198 Bgal/d (↓14% from 2010), groundwater 82.3 Bgal/d (↑8% from 2010).
*   **Saline sources**: Saline surface water 38.6 Bgal/d (↓14% from 2010), saline groundwater 2.34 Bgal/d (mostly mining).
*   **Water use by category (2015)**:
    *   Thermoelectric power: 133 Bgal/d (largest use; lowest since pre-1970).
    *   Irrigation: 118 Bgal/d (↑2% from 2010; second largest use).
    *   Public supply: 39.0 Bgal/d (↓7% from 2010).
    *   Self-supplied industrial: 14.8 Bgal/d (↓9% from 2010; continuing decline from 47 Bgal/d peak in 1970).
    *   Aquaculture: 7.55 Bgal/d (↓16% from 2010; 79% from surface water).
    *   Mining: 4.00 Bgal/d (~1% of total withdrawals).
    *   Livestock: 2.00 Bgal/d (unchanged from 2010).
*   **Public supply sources (2015)**: 23,800 Mgal/d from surface water, 15,200 Mgal/d from groundwater. About 26% of total U.S. water came from groundwater.
*   **State highlights (2015)**:
    *   12 states accounted for >50% of total withdrawals: California, Texas, Idaho, Florida, Arkansas, New York, Illinois, Colorado, North Carolina, Michigan, Montana, Nebraska.
    *   California: ~9% of total U.S. withdrawals and 9% of freshwater withdrawals (mainly irrigation).
    *   Texas: ~7% of total withdrawals (mainly thermoelectric, irrigation, public supply).
    *   Florida: 23% of total U.S. saline-water withdrawals (mostly surface water for thermoelectric).
    *   Texas & California: together 59% of total saline groundwater withdrawals (mostly mining).
*   **Historical trend (1950–2015, Bgal/d total)**: 184 (1950) → 271 (1960) → 372 (1970) → 435 (1980 peak) → 396 (1985) → 416 (2000) → 410 (2005) → 355 (2010) → 322 (2015). A full table with freshwater/saline breakdown is provided in the source.
*   **Data collection frequency**: USGS compiles county, state, and national water withdrawal and use data every 5 years.

**Flag**: Data reflects 2015 withdrawals, published in 2018. As of mid-2025, this is the latest complete USGS national water-use summary presented on this page, but it is a decade old. The next 5-year report (likely for 2020) may be available; verify for more current figures.
