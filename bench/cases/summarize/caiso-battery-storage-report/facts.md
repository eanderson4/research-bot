# Notes: 2024 Special Report on Battery Storage (CAISO, May 29, 2025)

## Publication details
- **Prepared by:** Department of Market Monitoring, California Independent System Operator
- **Date:** May 29, 2025
- **Coverage period:** 2024, with historical comparisons

---

## Key findings

- **CAISO balancing area battery capacity** grew from ~500 MW in 2020 to **13,000 MW** in December 2024. Over half is co‑located or hybrid with solar/wind generation.
- **WEIM battery capacity** outside the CAISO balancing area reached **~5,000 MW** by end‑2024 (vs 2,600 MW in Dec. 2023).
- Batteries provided **8.6% of CAISO energy** during net‑peak hours 17–21 (2024 average).
- Battery charging represented **14.7% of load** during hours‑ending 10–13 (peak solar hours).
- Batteries supplied **84% of the ISO’s regulation up and down** requirements.
- **Net market revenue** fell from **$78/kW‑yr** in 2023 to **$53/kW‑yr** in 2024, largely due to lower peak prices and loads.
- Batteries received **$17.9 million** in real‑time bid cost recovery (BCR), **11%** of all BCR paid to generators, and **4%** of total battery net revenues.
- **Local market power mitigation** had minimal dispatch impact: ~363 MW/hr had bids lowered, potentially increasing dispatch by **~35 MW/hr**.
- Some RA‑obligated battery capacity was **unavailable** during consecutive tight hours because of duration limits, modeling limitations, and bidding behavior.

---

## Installed capacity

- **Dec. 2024 CAISO BA:** 13,000 MW active capacity (5,800 MW stand‑alone, 5,700 MW co‑located, ~1,500 MW storage component of hybrids). Aggregate energy capacity: **47,300 MWh**.
- As of **Jan. 1, 2025**, limited‑energy storage resources made up **nearly 14%** of CAISO nameplate capacity.
- **Interconnection queue (Q2 2025):** batteries account for **46%** of total queue capacity.
- Sizes range from **470 kW to 325 MW**; most are **4‑hour duration**.
- **WEIM (outside CAISO BA):** 50 active battery resources, ~5,000 MW discharge capacity, **16,700 MWh** max SOC. Batteries were **5%** of WEIM outside CAISO nameplate in 2024, ~**9%** by Jan. 1, 2025.
- **Planned capacity (EIA March 2025 inventory, 2025‑2028):**  
  – California: **8,230 MW**  
  – Other WEIM states: **19,350 MW**  
  – Arizona 35%, Nevada 14%, Oregon+Washington ~30% of non‑CA planned additions.

---

## Energy bids and pricing

- **Day‑ahead bid spread (2024):** charge bids avg **$81/MWh** below nodal price; discharge bids avg **$230/MWh** above; spread **$312/MWh** (vs $235 in 2023).
- **Real‑time bid spread (2024):** **$223/MWh** (vs $202 in 2023).
- In Q3 2024, most battery capacity was bid at **<$100/MWh**, though some was bid at the $1,000/MWh cap.
- Bid curves showed **low responsiveness** to day‑ahead price changes compared to 2023 (Figures 2.7–2.8).

---

## Market schedules and ancillary services

- **5‑minute energy schedules:** peak discharge rose to **5,700 MW in HE 19** in 2024 (2,700 MW in 2023).
- **Ancillary services:** batteries provided 84% of regulation; share of total battery capacity scheduled for ancillary services has **declined** as capacity grew.
- **2024:** 9% of battery capacity scheduled for upward reg/spin/non‑spin (down from 12% in 2023).
- Battery charging was **14.7% of load** in hours‑ending 10–13.

---

## WEIM battery operations

- WEIM batteries are **self‑scheduled mainly via base schedules**, with minimal real‑time incremental dispatch.
- **Net real‑time revenues for non‑CAISO BA batteries in 2024: negative $8.8 million**. Estimated energy value using base schedules and LMPs: **~$32/kW‑year**.
- Minimum ancillary service participation (no ancillary service market in WEIM entities).

---

## Co‑located and hybrid resources

- **Co‑located model:** 76 sets active, 7 single‑technology.  
  – Cost savings: NREL (2022) estimated **7% lower cost** than separate siting.  
  – Federal Investment Tax Credit extended to stand‑alone storage (Inflation Reduction Act).  
  – New parameter (Nov. 2023): optional **off‑grid charging indicator**; max observed restricted charging **~750 MW** in 2024.  
  – Co‑located batteries deliver **more energy and less ancillary services per MW** than stand‑alone; benefit from lower LMPs at solar‑heavy nodes.
- **Hybrid model (battery + generation as one resource):** 35 active hybrids with storage (CAISO+WEIM).  
  – 96% registered as NGRs; only ~40% eligible for grid charging; **very few market awards to charge from grid**.  
  – Dynamic limits implemented **Feb. 2023**: operators update real‑time capabilities every 5 minutes for a 6‑hour look‑ahead.

---

## Market revenue and bid cost recovery

- **Total net battery revenues (CAISO BA)** up ~20% in 2024 due to larger fleet, but normalized to **$53/kW‑yr** (down from $78 in 2023). Energy contributed **82%** of revenues.
- **Bid cost recovery:**  
  – Batteries received **$17.9 million** real‑time BCR in 2024, **11%** of all generator BCR.  
  – BCR payments are largely from real‑time reversal of day‑ahead positions caused by state‑of‑charge limitations.  
  – DMM and Market Surveillance Committee note the design **incents inefficient bidding, removes real‑time price exposure, and creates gaming opportunities**. DMM recommends reform.

---

## Resource adequacy

- **CPUC reforms (2025):** “slice‑of‑day” requires load‑serving entities to show capacity for all 24 hours on the “worst day” each month; storage excess capacity test ensures batteries can be fully charged over four critical net‑peak hours.
- **NQC:** based on 4‑hour sustained output; no extra credit for longer durations. Hybrid/co‑located storage gets less NQC than identically sized stand‑alone.
- **2024 RA participation:** **65–94%** of battery NQC counted toward RA, varying by month.
- **Availability on high‑demand days:**  
  – On the 5 highest‑load days, after accounting for outages/de‑rates, scheduled battery capacity in HE 20 exceeded 100% of RA capacity.  
  – **Undispatched capacity** included bids above LMP (opportunity pricing) and bids below LMP that were not dispatched due to constraints.  
  – During the July 24, 2024 EEA Watch hours, in HE 22 **19%** of capacity was bid below LMP but not dispatched.
- **Factors limiting availability:** limited optimization horizon, ASSOC, aggregate capability constraint, biddable SOC parameters, attenuation, physical foldback/cell imbalance (average **5% of nameplate charge range** was on outage in 2024).

---

## Local market power mitigation

- Batteries subject to LMPM since **Nov. 2021** (exempt if ≤5 MW and parent is non‑net‑supplier). Mitigation applies to both charging and discharging bids.
- **Storage Default Energy Bid (DEB):**  
  `DEB = Max(Min(En·∂/η,0)+ρ, OC)·1.1`  
  – OC: Nth highest day‑ahead LMP, where N = discharge duration (typically 4). Real‑time DEB uses day‑ahead LMPs for the trade date.  
- **Mitigation impact (2024):**  
  – 15‑minute market: avg potential dispatch increase **40 MW/hr**; bids lowered on **363 MW/hr**; actual dispatch increase **~35 MW/hr**.  
  – Day‑ahead: avg **19 MW**; 5‑minute: avg **45 MW** potential increase.  
- **Hybrid resources** are **exempt** from mitigation regardless of size, though some could hypothetically be pivotal.
- DMM proposes **hourly‑varying DEB** to better capture real‑time opportunity cost.

---

## Technical & market design details noted

- **Non‑Generator Resource (NGR) model:** single supply curve, energy limits, optional EOH SOC parameter (approved FERC May 2021, real‑time only).
- **Multi‑interval optimization horizons:** day‑ahead 24 hrs; 15‑minute market 2 hrs ahead; 5‑minute market 13 intervals (65 min).
- **MSOC requirement** (temporary) expired Sep. 2023; replaced by enhanced exceptional dispatch for batteries.
- **Attenuation factors** for regulation impact on SOC updated quarterly.
- **Aggregate capability constraint (ACC)** for co‑located resources.
- **Outage Management System:** batteries may restrict upper/lower SOC through OMS; 5% of nameplate charge range on outage in 2024.
